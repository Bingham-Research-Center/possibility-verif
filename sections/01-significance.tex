\section*{Significance Statement}
\begingroup
\hyphenpenalty=10000
\exhyphenpenalty=10000
Possibilistic forecasts encode richer uncertainty than probabilities---they
distinguish ``this outcome is merely plausible'' from ``this outcome is
essentially certain'' while explicitly flagging when the forecasting system
lacks knowledge.  However, no systematic method exists to verify whether
such forecasts are sharp, honest, and useful.  This paper fills that gap
with a five-number scorecard that evaluates each forecast on its own
possibilistic terms, a principled conversion to probabilities that
preserves the ignorance signal for information-gain scoring, and
categorical threshold scores that connect to familiar operational metrics.
Together, these three verification facets give forecast developers and
end-users the diagnostic tools to distinguish honestly uncertain forecasts
from confidently wrong ones---and to measure, compare, and improve
possibilistic prediction systems.
\endgroup
