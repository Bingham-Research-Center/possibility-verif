\section*{Significance Statement}
\begingroup
\hyphenpenalty=10000
\exhyphenpenalty=10000
A probabilistic forecast must sum to unity, so it cannot express ``I
don't know.''  A possibilistic forecast can: its subnormality explicitly
flags how much of the plausibility budget the system left unassigned.
This paper provides the first verification framework for such forecasts,
built around a five-number scorecard that separately diagnoses whether the
forecast pointed at the right outcome, how sharply, and how honestly.
A principled conversion to probabilities preserves the ignorance signal
for information-gain scoring, and familiar categorical threshold metrics
(POD, FAR, CSI) connect to operational practice.  Together, these three
verification facets let forecast developers and end-users distinguish
honestly uncertain forecasts from confidently wrong ones---and measure
where a possibilistic system is improving and where it is not.
\endgroup
