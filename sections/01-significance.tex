\section*{Significance Statement}
\begingroup
\hyphenpenalty=10000
\exhyphenpenalty=10000
A probabilistic forecast must sum to unity and cannot express ``I
don't know.'' A possibilistic forecast can: a subnormal distribution quantifies how much of the plausibility budget the system left unassigned. A verification framework for such forecasts is built around a five-number scorecard that separately diagnoses whether the
forecast pointed at the right outcome, how sharply, and how honestly.
A conversion to probabilities preserves the ignorance signal
for information-gain scoring, and familiar categorical threshold metrics
(POD, FAR, CSI) connect to operational practice. Combined, this multifaceted evaluation distinguishes
honestly uncertain forecasts from confidently wrong ones: this scorecard measures
where a possibilistic system is improving in priority areas.
\endgroup
